Shymkent

Shymkent was founded in the twelfth century as a caravanserai to protect a nearby Silk Road trade town, Sayram, 10 km to the east. Shymkent grew as a market center for trade between Turkic nomads and the settled Sogdians.

Geographic location

Aqmeshit Cave is known as the biggest cave in Central Asia and appears to be a sacred place for all comers who travel there to make a wish. Aqmeshit Cave is located 90 km away from Shymkent city. The cave is 32-34 m high, the length is 162 m, and the width is 64 m
